About This Service

A Smarter Alternative to a Full Remodel

Not every kitchen or bathroom needs to be ripped down to the studs. Many Montgomery County homeowners are surprised by how much impact targeted updates can make — a new vanity and mirror in the guest bath, a tile backsplash behind the stove, updated cabinet hardware, or a fresh coat of moisture-resistant paint in the right color. These changes can take a dated room and make it feel contemporary for a fraction of a full renovation budget.

Leo General Contracting specializes in these strategic refreshes. We work with you to identify the changes that will have the biggest visual and functional impact, then execute them efficiently so your kitchen or bathroom is back in service quickly. From a half-bath update in your Chevy Chase bungalow to a full kitchen refresh in your North Bethesda split-level, we bring the same craftsmanship to every project.

Bathroom & Kitchen Refresh Questions

A full remodel typically involves gutting the room — tearing out walls, moving plumbing, replacing subfloor, and reconfiguring the layout. A refresh works within the existing footprint and focuses on cosmetic and functional upgrades: swapping the vanity, adding tile, updating fixtures, painting, and replacing hardware. It costs significantly less, takes days instead of weeks, and still delivers a dramatic transformation.
A simple refresh — painting, hardware swap, and new fixtures — can be done in 1–2 days. A more involved project with a vanity replacement, backsplash tile, and flooring typically takes 3–5 days. We schedule the work to minimize downtime so your kitchen or bathroom is usable as quickly as possible.
