About This Service

Upgrade Your Bathroom Without Tearing It Apart

A bathroom doesn't need to be stripped to the studs to feel like new. Replacing an outdated vanity, installing a clean tile surround in the shower, swapping old faucets and showerheads for modern fixtures, and adding better lighting can completely change the look and function of the space. These targeted updates work within your existing layout and plumbing, keeping costs down and timelines short while still delivering a dramatic improvement you'll notice every morning.

Leo General Contracting handles every detail of your bathroom refresh — from proper waterproofing behind tile surrounds to exhaust fan upgrades that protect against moisture and mold. We make sure supply lines and drain connections are sound before installing new fixtures, apply mold-resistant finishes in high-humidity areas, and seal every joint with precision caulking and grout work. Whether it's a powder room update in Potomac or a full hall bath refresh in Rockville, we bring the same attention to craftsmanship and moisture management to every project.

Bathroom Refresh Questions

Most bathroom refreshes take between 2 and 5 days depending on the scope of work. A straightforward vanity swap with new fixtures and paint can be completed in 1-2 days. A more involved project that includes tile surround installation, flooring, and multiple fixture replacements typically takes 3-5 days. We'll give you a clear timeline during your free estimate so you know exactly how long your bathroom will be out of service.
Yes. Our bathroom refresh services include connecting new faucets, showerheads, and toilets to your existing supply and drain lines. We replace shut-off valves, supply lines, and wax rings as needed during fixture installations. If we discover any underlying plumbing issues during the refresh, we'll let you know and discuss your options before proceeding.
We'll do our best to find a close match if you want to extend or patch existing tile. In many cases, we can source tile that blends well with what's already in your bathroom. If an exact match isn't available, we can suggest complementary accent tiles or a design approach that incorporates the new tile in a way that looks intentional rather than patched. We'll bring samples so you can compare before we commit.
Proper waterproofing is critical in any bathroom project. Before installing tile on shower or tub surrounds, we apply a waterproof membrane to the backer board to prevent moisture from reaching the wall structure behind it. All seams, corners, and fixture penetrations are sealed. We use mold-resistant grout and apply quality silicone caulk at all change-of-plane joints. This approach prevents the hidden water damage and mold growth that can result from shortcuts.
