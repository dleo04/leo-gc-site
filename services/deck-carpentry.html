About This Service

Outdoor Living Spaces and Interior Woodwork You Can Count On

A well-built deck extends your living space into the backyard and adds real value to your home. But Maryland's humid summers, cold winters, and heavy rain mean that deck materials and construction methods matter. Leo General Contracting builds decks designed for the Montgomery County climate — properly graded, sealed, and fastened to stand up to years of weather without warping, splitting, or rot.

Inside the home, our finish carpentry services handle the woodwork details that elevate a room: crown molding, baseboards, window and door casings, wainscoting, and custom built-in shelving. Whether you're adding a new composite deck to your Potomac home or installing new trim throughout your Rockville rancher, we measure twice, cut precisely, and finish cleanly.

Deck & Carpentry Questions

Composite decking (like Trex or TimberTech) is the most popular choice for Montgomery County homes because it resists moisture, won't splinter, and requires almost no maintenance. Pressure-treated lumber is a budget-friendly option that holds up well when properly sealed every 1–2 years. We'll walk you through the pros, cons, and costs of each option during your estimate.
A standard 12x16 deck typically takes 5–10 days from start to finish, depending on the design complexity, material availability, and weather. Multi-level decks, pergola additions, or built-in seating add time. We'll provide a detailed timeline during the planning phase so you know exactly what to expect.
Yes. Most new deck builds in Montgomery County require a building permit. We handle the permit application process, ensure the design meets all local building codes, and coordinate any required inspections. You don't have to deal with the county paperwork — that's part of our service.
For pressure-treated wood decks in Maryland, we recommend staining and sealing every 1–2 years to protect against UV damage and moisture. Composite decks need much less maintenance — typically just periodic cleaning. We offer deck staining and sealing as a standalone service if your existing deck just needs a refresh.
