About This Service

New Floors That Transform Every Room

Your floors set the tone for your entire home, and choosing the right material makes all the difference. Hardwood remains a top choice for Montgomery County homeowners who want timeless warmth and lasting value — we handle both new hardwood installation and refinishing of existing floors to bring out their natural beauty. For kitchens, bathrooms, basements, and other moisture-prone areas, luxury vinyl plank offers the look of real wood with waterproof durability that stands up to Maryland's humid summers and wet winters.

If you're looking for an affordable upgrade that still delivers a polished look, laminate flooring provides excellent durability and scratch resistance at a budget-friendly price point — perfect for high-traffic hallways and living areas. For bedrooms and cozy spaces, nothing beats the comfort of new carpet underfoot. Leo General Contracting installs all four flooring types across Montgomery County, from Potomac colonials to Rockville townhomes, handling every step from old floor removal and subfloor preparation to trim finishing and final cleanup.

Flooring Installation Questions

Maryland's hot, humid summers and cold winters cause wood to expand and contract throughout the year. Luxury vinyl plank (LVP) is the most humidity-resistant option — it's 100% waterproof and won't warp or buckle. Engineered hardwood is another strong choice, as its layered construction handles moisture swings better than solid hardwood. For basements and below-grade spaces, we typically recommend LVP or laminate with a moisture barrier underlayment. We'll assess your specific rooms and recommend the best fit.
For a standard-sized room (around 200 square feet), most flooring installations take 1–2 days. Laminate and luxury vinyl plank go in the fastest — often in a single day. Hardwood installation takes a bit longer, especially if the subfloor needs leveling or the boards need to acclimate to your home's humidity for 48–72 hours before installation. Carpet is typically the quickest, often completed in a few hours per room. We'll give you an accurate timeline during your free estimate.
In many cases, yes. Luxury vinyl plank and laminate can often be installed directly over existing hard flooring as long as the surface is flat, clean, and in good condition. However, we don't recommend installing over carpet, damaged flooring, or surfaces with moisture issues. Installing over existing flooring can save time and money by avoiding removal costs, but it does raise the floor height slightly, which can affect door clearance and transitions. We'll inspect your current floors and let you know the best approach.
We can handle everything — sourcing quality hardwood, LVP, laminate, or carpet along with underlayment, transition strips, and trim, all included in your estimate. If you've already purchased materials or prefer to shop on your own, we're happy to install whatever you provide. We work with trusted local and national suppliers and can help you compare options, request samples, and find the best value for your budget.
