About This Service

Tile Installation That Looks Right and Lasts

Good tile work is the difference between a bathroom that feels like a spa and one that looks like a DIY weekend project. The key is preparation — a level substrate, proper waterproofing in wet areas, consistent spacing, and clean cuts around outlets, edges, and fixtures. At Leo General Contracting, we bring the patience and precision that tile demands to every installation in Montgomery County.

Whether you're updating a powder room floor in your Bethesda Cape Cod, installing a herringbone backsplash in your Gaithersburg kitchen, or building a complete walk-in shower surround in your Silver Spring split-level, we work with ceramic, porcelain, natural stone, glass, and mosaic tile. We'll help you choose the right tile for the application and install it so it looks beautiful for decades.

Tile Work Questions & Answers

Porcelain tile is the go-to choice for bathroom floors because it's dense, water-resistant, and extremely durable. For safety, we recommend textured or matte finishes that provide grip when wet. Smaller tiles (like hexagon or mosaic) also work well because the extra grout lines add traction. We'll help you choose a tile that balances style and safety.
A typical bathroom floor (around 40–60 square feet) takes 2–3 days — one day for prep and setting tile, a day for grout curing, and a final day for grouting and cleanup. Larger areas like kitchens or entryways take proportionally longer. We factor in curing time so the finished floor is ready for foot traffic.

We'll assess your specific situation during the estimate.
Porcelain and ceramic tile are very low maintenance — regular sweeping and mopping with a mild cleaner is all that's needed. For natural stone tile, we recommend sealing every 1–2 years. Grout should be sealed after installation and resealed periodically to prevent staining. We'll give you specific care instructions for your tile type when the job is complete.
